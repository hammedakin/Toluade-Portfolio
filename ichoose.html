ICHOOSE

Ichoose is a project that explores the difficulty of living and working in Nigeria. It seeks to know the pain of people in their various career paths, their expectations from the past government and its failures. Most importantly, it opens up the conversations around the 2019 election, the expectations of the citizens and the reason why they should vote.

ICHOOSE

It's saddening to find many nurses unemployed even when our hospitals are understaffed and bulk of the citizenry underserved. Private hospitals employ more nurses but they do not pay so well. We tend to take care of more patients than we should, imagine a nurse to 22 patients or more instead of the standard one to 4 patients. All these affect the quality of nursing care. Many times we do not have access to equipments to render proper service to patients. I hope with my PVC to elect leaders who are passionate about health care. Leaders who will provide primary health care in every community so the secondary and tertiary health care institutions can be decongested. Also, affordable health care services should be provided for all.

ICHOOSE

I am a Masters student of the University of Ibadan and a part time teacher. I am very passionate about teaching but for some years now, the government has not employed teachers. So we are left at the mercies of the private schools that use us so much and yet pay very little. My first salary was 7,500 after graduating from the University and still it did not come regularly. My mum works with the state government and salaries do not come often so I have to help my siblings even with the little I make. I am going to choose with my PVC a government that cares, I know that nobody has the capacity to change things overnight but sympathy and empathy for the plight of Nigerians is key for a good government.

ICHOOSE

I am a software engineer and the basic things I need to work are basically power and data. These things are either expensive or unavailable. The world is moving fast and we have a responsibility to bring solution to our immediate society yet we have to battle the limitations around us. Another issue is that we cannot look good on the street because of harassment from SARS yet we cannot look unkempt because clients will not take you serious. Been a Nigerian means you are a second class citizen compared to your peers in the world. Nothing is free here except the air we breathe which is polluted. I have not lost hope in Nigeria and with my PVC I will vote. It's not about winning for any candidate I vote for, it is the satisfaction I get from performing my duty as a Nigerian. I feel beyond complaining, the polling booth is the next place to go to.

I am a music producer/mixing engineer. Getting equipments is difficult in Nigeria because of the dollar rise. Going through school as an undergraduate was not easy at all. There was usually no light, no water, during exams.
